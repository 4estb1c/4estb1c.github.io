Haircut — Apgujeong salon ~$35–90
Apgujeong is where Korean hair happens, and it's the one neighbourhood where English-speaking stylists are normal rather than a favour. Expect a long consultation before anyone touches your head, a scalp wash, and a blow-dry-and-style included in the price.

Gyeongbokgung Palace — free entry in hanbok Free
The largest of the five Joseon palaces and the throne of the dynasty: Geunjeongjeon hall, the pavilion floating on Gyeonghoeru pond, the restored quarters behind. Free while you're in hanbok. The changing-of-the-guard ceremony at the main gate runs at 10:00 and 14:00.
Go straight to the back half of the grounds first — the crowds cluster at the front third and never make it past the throne hall. The pond pavilion at the northwest corner is the photo everyone wants and it's usually empty before 11.

Bukchon Hanok Village Free
The hillside of preserved hanok streets between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung, still lived in — which is the whole point and also the whole etiquette problem. Walk up Bukchon-ro 11-gil for the famous roofline-over-city view, then let yourself get lost in the lanes.
People genuinely live here and the signs asking for quiet are not decorative — residents have successfully lobbied to close streets to tourists before. Keep your voice down and don't photograph doorways. Hanbok makes you extremely visible; be the good version of that.

A whole young chicken stuffed with glutinous rice, ginseng, jujube and garlic, simmered into a milky restorative broth. Seoul's most famous version, in a hanok compound behind the palace. It is enormous, it is basically pure protein, and it's the correct thing to eat in July — Koreans eat it in the hottest weeks specifically.

Clear beef short-rib soup — a gentler cousin of gomtang, with the ribs falling off the bone into the broth. Understated and very good, and about as protein-dense as lunch gets.

pick one — cheapest and best-value first
Gel nail studios (네일샵) — Insadong / Jongno ~$25–55
The one to do if you only do one. Korean nail art is genuinely the best in the world and roughly half US pricing: ₩35,000 for a clean gel manicure, ₩45,000–70,000 with real art, about ₩70,000 for mani + pedi together. Sixty to ninety minutes, and the design work — chrome, jelly, tiny hand-painted detail — is a level above anything you'll get at home. Insadong is a 10-min walk from Bukchon and has several.
Skin-care shop facial (피부관리실) ~$40–70
Exactly the "facial massage and treatment" thing: 60–90 minutes of double cleanse, steam, extractions, a long face-neck-shoulder massage, sheet mask and LED, for ₩50,000–90,000. Non-medical, unhurried, and what Korean women actually book monthly — not a hotel-spa ceremony. Look for 피부관리실 or 에스테틱 on the sign; the blocks between Insadong and Jongno 3-ga have a run of them.
Head spa (두피 스파) — JUNO Hair Myeongdong ~$45–90
Korea's current obsession, and the most relaxing hour on this entire itinerary: scalp diagnosis under a microscope, deep cleanse, steam, a very long scalp-neck-shoulder massage, treatment mask, then a blow-out. From about ₩60,000 for an entry-level course. JUNO is the same chain as Thursday's haircut, so one account books both — and Myeongdong is a stop from the hotel.
Aqua peel / hydrafacial at a skin clinic ~$50–90
The fast, clinical version: vacuum-and-serum resurfacing that clears pores and leaves skin visibly brighter, in 30–45 minutes rather than 90. Less pampering, more result. Myeongdong and Hongdae clinics are set up for tourists with multilingual staff. Note the medical tax-refund scheme ended in Dec 2025, so the quoted price is the price.
Jjimjilbang + seshin body scrub (Dragon Hill, Yongsan) ~$35
The cheapest and most authentically Korean of the lot: ₩15,000 entry to a 24-hour bathhouse with hot pools, salt rooms, jade saunas and cold plunges, plus about ₩30,000 for a full-body seshin — an ajumma scrubbing a layer off you on a vinyl table, followed by a massage. Brutal, hilarious, and you will never feel cleaner. It's also directly on the route to Yeouido.
Sulwhasoo Spa (Bukchon flagship) ~$120–300
Left in for completeness if you'd rather splurge after all: ginseng hanbang treatments from Korea's heritage house, in a flagship built like a gallery. The most indulgent facial in the city, and priced like it.
