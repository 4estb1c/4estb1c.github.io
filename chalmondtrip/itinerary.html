Yongsan / Hannam
10:00
Dragon Hill Spa — jjimjilbang + scrub ~$25
Korea’s most famous jimjilbang in Yongsan (10 min from Hongdae). ₩12,000 entry covers the full complex — hot/cold pools, mugwort steam, charcoal sauna. Add a body scrub (Italian-towel scrub, ₩20,000) for a deeply Korean experience. Plan 2—3 hours.

Michelin-listed kalguksu (knife-cut noodle soup) and mandu. The Itaewon branch has shorter queues than the Myeongdong original.
Naengmyeon / cold noodles (post-spa) ~$11
After 2—3 hours in the sauna, a bowl of icy mul-naengmyeon (chilled buckwheat noodles) is exactly right. Several Hannam and Itaewon spots do a proper version. Cooling, light, and very Korean.
Local gimbap / jeongsik spots (side alleys) ~$10
Hannam’s back alleys have cheap set-meal restaurants (baekban) with banchan spreads. Ask your hotel; they’ll point you to the best block.
14:00
National Museum of Korea Free
10 min from Dragon Hill in Yongsan: one of the largest museums in the world, and free. Korean history and art from antiquity through the Joseon dynasty, beautifully staged — the ten-storey Gyeongcheonsa Pagoda rising indoors, the gold crowns of Silla, and the hushed room of the Pensive Bodhisattva. A reflecting pool and pagoda garden outside. Budget two hours; it pairs perfectly with the morning spa for a calm, restorative day.
Closed Mondays — fine, you're here on a Sunday. The Buddhist sculpture hall and the Silla gold are the highlights if you're short on time.

Outdoor · open-air
  • Tamar Park (Admiralty) — harbourfront lawn and benches with the full skyline in front of you, and a breeze off the water. ~10 min from the hotel.
